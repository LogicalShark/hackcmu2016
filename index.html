Modeling and Analysis of Financial Markets in MapleStory

Problem


One of the major difficulties facing modern economics is the inability to conduct experiments. Often, economists have to relied on computer simulations as a medium to conduct financial and economic experiments on subjects varying from Nash Equilibrium to Gale-Shapley algorithms. However, data is sometimes limited due to the short the frame in which they are run and the often limited participants.


Solution


One of the ways economics have been countering this problem is by studying massively multiplayer online role-playing. This solves two of the major problems faced by current simulations. With over 8 million users, the number is large enough to ensure that outliers do not affect the data set. In addition, with the game over a decade old, this is longer than any economic experiment run.
